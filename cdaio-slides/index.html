What is an AI agent?

Build it up one click at a time, narrate each step. (1) Base: an LLM is just text in, text out. A user message goes in, a reply comes out. (2) Add the conversation so far: now it remembers, that is basically ChatGPT. (3) Add a system message (instructions, plus information it should know): now you control its behavior. (4) Give it a tool: instead of replying straight away it can call the weather tool, get the forecast back, then answer for real. That loop is what makes it an agent. Sparse on purpose, I say the rest out loud.

The ONLY three things you control

The model
The LLM, the large language model: the brain. Bigger and smarter, or cheaper and faster. Hosted by a provider, or run on your own machines. Many trade-offs.
The instructions
The system message. Information it was never trained on, and the rules you want it to follow.
The tools
What it can reach and act on. Like you, but with your phone in your hand.
The three knobs from the diagram. The model is the brain: bigger vs cheaper, hosted by an AI company vs run locally, many trade-offs, but I cover this fast out loud. The system message is where you personalize: the information it lacks, and more interestingly the instructions you want it to follow. The tools are what it can act on, the "you without your phone" line. Minimal text on purpose, I narrate.

Two ways to put AI to work

An AI project

Using an AI assistant (ChatGPT, Claude, Claude Code) to help you work, or to work for you.

One-off, and AI is here to augment your productivity and your capabilities.

An AI-powered process / workflow

Built to be repeated, and to play a big role in the company.

Like any workflow in manufacturing or software.

And here is the surprise: most of the work is already yours.
95% your domain knowledge: operations, your business, how work gets organized
5% AI: one new ingredient you fold into how you already organize work.
Two ways to use AI. A project is using an assistant, ChatGPT or Claude or Claude Code, to help you work or to work for you, a one-off that is here to augment your productivity and your capabilities. A process or workflow is the other thing: built to be repeated, to play a big role, like any workflow in manufacturing or software. Now the surprise, and this is about that second kind, the workflow you just built: building one of these processes is about ninety-five percent your domain knowledge, how your business and your operations actually work, and only about five percent AI. AI is just one new ingredient you fold into how you already organize things. You do not need to become an AI engineer. You need the judgment you already have, plus this one new thing. This is the genuinely new question, the one that did not exist a few years ago. You now have a new option in the picture: hand the messy, judgment-heavy work to AI. Two axes. Across: how ambiguous is the task. Up: how costly a mistake is. Clear and cheap, just automate it, no AI. Clear but costly, automate with checks. Messy but low stakes, let the AI run it. Messy and costly, keep a human in the loop, and design that human as a guardrail, not a bottleneck. The deeper point: you do not need to work for an AI company to reason about this. You need the intuition for what is now possible, and the only way to get it is to use the tools, like we just did. What’s really going on at Proxima

A communication problem between the field and headquarters, and AI is the proposed fix.

Build it up one click at a time. Base: reps are in the field, they see what is really going on; management has to make the calls. (1) Directives flow top-down. (2) The CRM is how the field is supposed to flow back up so management can decide. (3) But logging is a chore for a busy rep, so it rarely happens, and HQ ends up flying blind: that is the real communication problem, and it is human, not technical. (4) AfterVisit AI captures every visit automatically and auto-fills the CRM, closing the loop. The pitch: we are not adding AI for its own sake, we are using it to fix a broken flow of information.

Building with AI

AI can do much more for you than run workflows, almost like your own “remote employee.”
Transition out of Maya and the company case. So far today we took a technology and leveraged it inside a defined workflow, the kind of repeatable process you could also staff with people. But a lot of what we actually do is not a neat, repeatable workflow. We have judgment, we have agency, we move across many different tasks in a day. So here is the question for this last part: what if AI could do that too, act less like a single tool wired into one process, and more like a general-purpose employee you can direct? That is where things are heading, and you can start using it today. Keep this part light and inspiring, it is the close.
