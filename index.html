I am an Assistant Professor in Operations at the Kellogg School of Management, Northwestern University.

My research is generally focused on the interface between humans and algorithms, with the hope of positively affecting society. Most of my work lie in the academic fields of analytics, mathematical modeling, transportation, and public policy.

I have a particular interest in the impact of my research on the real world. For example, I helped Boston redesign its school transportation systen, San Francisco change its school schedules, and Lyft change its matching algorithm. These changes save tens of millions of dollars every year and benefit millions of people, they were recognize twice as Franz Edelman Award laureates.
